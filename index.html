Oha soup is a traditional Igbo soup from Nigeria. It is made with oha leaves, which have a unique flavor that adds a delightful taste to the soup. Oha soup is typically cooked with a combination of meats, such as beef, goat meat, or assorted meats, and is seasoned with local spices and herbs. Some variations of the soup may also include ingredients like cocoyam or ugu leaves. The result is a rich and flavorful soup that is often enjoyed with fufu or pounded yam. Afang soup is a delicious Nigerian soup that originates from the Efik and Ibibio tribes. It is made with afang leaves, which have a unique flavor, and is typically cooked with a combination of meats, fish, and other ingredients like periwinkle, crayfish, and palm oil. The result is a rich and flavorful soup that is often enjoyed with fufu or pounded yam. Afang soup is known for its distinct taste and the nutritional value it provides. Okra soup is a versatile vegetable that is commonly used in cooking. It is known for its slimy texture when cooked, which can be a turn-off for some people, but others enjoy it. Okra is often used in soups, stews, and stir-fries, and it can also be pickled or fried. It's a popular ingredient in many cuisines around the world, including Nigerian cuisine. In Nigeria, okra is commonly used to make dishes like okra soup, also known as "okro soup," which is often enjoyed with fufu or rice. If you enjoy trying new flavors and textures, give okra a try!"OKRA".


Egusi soup is a popular Nigerian dish made from ground melon seeds. It's often cooked with a variety of vegetables, meats, and spices, creating a rich and flavorful soup. The melon seeds give the soup a thick and creamy texture, while the combination of ingredients adds depth to the taste. Egusi soup is commonly enjoyed with pounded yam, eba, or fufu.
